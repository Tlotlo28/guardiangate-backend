1.Who we are

GuardianGate is a school-safety learner-pickup platform operated by Senelo Africa Group Holdings ("we", "us", "GuardianGate"). This policy explains how we handle personal information in line with South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA).

It applies to the GuardianGate mobile app and the GuardianGate school dashboard, used by participating schools, their staff and guards, and parents or guardians.

2.What information we collect

We collect only what is needed to run a safe pickup system:

  • Learner details: the child's name and grade, and a unique QR code that identifies them.
  • Parent / guardian details: name, email address, phone number, and the relationship to the learner (e.g. mother, father, authorised driver).
  • Staff & guard details: name, email, and role for the people who operate the system at the school.
  • Pickup activity: a record of each pickup — which learner, which collector, which guard, and the date and time — kept as a safety log.
  • Basic technical data: a secure login token stored on your own device to keep you signed in.

We do not collect location tracking, photographs of children, financial information, or any data beyond what the pickup process requires.

3.Why we collect it & our lawful basis

We use this information only to:

  • Verify that a person collecting a learner is approved to do so;
  • Let a parent approve or deny a pickup from their own phone;
  • Keep an accurate safety record of who collected each child and when;
  • Share school notices and let parents report absences.

Under POPIA, our lawful basis is the protection of the safety of the child, the legitimate interests of the school and parents in a secure pickup process, and the consent of the parent or guardian who registers to use the app.

4.Children's information (special protection)

POPIA treats information about children as special personal information that deserves extra protection. We only process a learner's information for the direct purpose of their safe collection, on the authority of the school and with the involvement of the child's parent or guardian.

A learner's QR code contains only a random identifier — it does not contain the child's name, location, or any readable personal detail. If a badge is lost, a new code can be issued and the old one deactivated.

5.Who can see the information

  • School administrators/staff can see the learners, guardians, and pickup records for their own school only.
  • Guards can see a learner's approved collectors at the moment of a scan, to verify a pickup.
  • Parents/guardians can only see their own children and their own pickup requests, notices, and absences.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share personal information with advertisers or unrelated third parties.

Information is kept separate per school, so one school can never see another school's data.

6.How we store & protect it

  • Passwords are never stored in readable form — they are protected using industry-standard one-way hashing (bcrypt).
  • All communication between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit (HTTPS).
  • Data is stored in a secured, access-controlled cloud database.
  • Access is limited by role, so each person only sees what their role allows.

No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable and appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your information, as POPIA requires, and we continue to strengthen these as the platform grows.

7.How long we keep it

We keep personal information only for as long as a learner or user is active with a participating school, plus a reasonable period afterwards for safety and record-keeping purposes. When a learner leaves or a user is removed, their record is deactivated and hidden from active use, and can be permanently deleted on request, subject to any legal record-keeping obligations.

8.Your rights under POPIA

As a parent or guardian, you have the right to:

  • Ask what information we hold about you and your child;
  • Correct information that is wrong or out of date;
  • Ask us to delete your information, where we are not legally required to keep it;
  • Withdraw consent to use the app (though this may mean the digital pickup service can no longer be provided for your child);
  • Complain to the Information Regulator of South Africa if you believe your rights have been infringed (inforegulator.org.za).

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Information Officer (details below).

9.Terms of Use

Acceptable use

GuardianGate is provided to participating schools and their registered parents, staff, and guards for the sole purpose of managing learner pickups and related school communication. You agree to use it only for this purpose and not to misuse, interfere with, or attempt to gain unauthorised access to the system.

Your account

You are responsible for keeping your login details private. Do not share your password. If you believe someone else has access to your account, contact the school office immediately so it can be reset.

Accuracy of information

Schools and parents are responsible for keeping learner and guardian details accurate and up to date, as these directly affect who is permitted to collect a child.

Availability

We aim to keep the service running reliably but do not guarantee uninterrupted availability. During the pilot phase, the manual code-entry option is always available as a fallback if the camera scan is unavailable on a particular device.

Changes to these terms

We may update this policy and these terms as the platform develops or as the law requires. Material changes will be communicated to participating schools.
